You need to ask the customer knowledge-based verification (KBV) questions

To pass the IDV check the customer needs to correctly answer 2 of 3 KBV questions. You must choose questions from 2 different systems, if available.

Use historical data where possible. For first-time claimants, do not use new claim data to verify their identity.

Questions from CIS Searchlight

  • "Name another benefit or DWP payment you receive." (A 'none' response is acceptable)
  • "Name another benefit you have claimed previously, regardless of whether it was paid to you?"
  • "What was your previous surname?"
  • "What were your previous addresses?" (First line of address and town are acceptable, postcode not needed).
  • "On which date did your claim for the other benefit start?" (The correct month and year is acceptable).
  • "What is the weekly or monthly amount of the other benefit?" (A reasonable tolerance range, depending on the amount, may be applied).
  • "When did you receive your last payment?"
  • "What is your partner’s National Insurance number?"
  • "What is your partner’s full name?"
  • "What is your partner’s date of birth?"
  • "How many dependents are in your claim?"
  • "What is the name of one of your dependent children?"
  • "What is the date of birth of one of your dependent children?"

Questions from legacy, PTP CAMS, UC systems

  • "What is the date of your most recent marriage?"
  • "Please provide your bank sort code where you receive benefits, pension, or primary income." (If this question is asked, the last 4 digits of bank account question should not be asked)
  • "What is the name of your primary home mortgage lender?"
  • "What is the monthly rate of your primary home mortgage payment?" (A reasonable range of difference may be applied depending on the amount).
  • "What is your pay or payment day?"
  • "What is the name of the employer that pays your work pension?"
  • "What is the weekly or monthly amount of your work pension?" (A reasonable tolerance range, depending on the amount, may be applied).
  • "What current deductions are taken from your benefit or wage?"
  • "How much is deducted from your benefit or wage?"(A reasonable tolerance range, depending on amount, can be applied).

The following questions can also be used to verify identity but only if not used as part of establishing identity:

  • "What is your home telephone number?" (Do not use this to verify identity in an outbound call if it is the number you have called the claimant on).
  • "What is your mobile telephone number?" (Do not use this to verify identity in an outbound call if it is the number you have called the claimant on).
  • "What is your email address?"

Questions that are business specific

  • "When did you last contact the department?" (No previous contact is acceptable).
  • "What was your last contact about?" (No previous contact is acceptable).
  • "What was the date of the recent correspondence you received from the department?" (The correct month and year is acceptable).
  • "What is your signing day?" (For relevant benefits).
  • "When were you last admitted or discharged from hospital?" (The correct month and year is acceptable).
  • "What is the name and surgery address of your GP?"

Any questions relating to a recent event.

  • "What components of PIP have you been awarded?" (Must give both components and levels if awarded).
  • "What other income or wage do you have?" (A reasonable tolerance range, depending on the amount, can be applied).
  • "What is the name and address of your previous employer?"
  • "What is your Debt Reference number?"
  • "On what date was your Vehicle Certificate of Entitlement issued?" (The correct month and year is acceptable).
  • "What is the name of the disabled person you care for?"
  • "What is the date of birth of the disabled person you care for?"
  • "What is your relationship with the disabled person you care for?"
  • "What is your Child Maintenance Group case reference number?"
